Introduction

Justice For India aims to document and raise awareness of the massive exploitation, systemic oppression, and crimes committed by the British colonial state in India. This includes economic drain, mass famines, forced labor, social disruption, and cultural destruction. This website also compares the British state’s methods with Nazi Germany to highlight state-led oppression and systematic injustice.

Colonial Crimes

Economic Exploitation

British systematically drained wealth from India from 1765 to 1938. Economist Utsa Patnaik estimates £9.2 trillion stolen, adjusted to ≈$45 trillion in modern value.

Mass Killings & Repression

Events such as Jallianwala Bagh massacre, 1857 suppression, and forced conscription in world wars caused millions of deaths.

Forced Labor & Cultural Destruction

Millions of Indians forced into plantations, railways, and army work without compensation; local arts, crafts, and industries collapsed.

Famine & Starvation

British policies worsened famines; Bengal Famine 1943 caused 3–5 million deaths due to export prioritization, hoarding, and war-time neglect.

Economic Loot

India’s GDP share fell from ~25% of world GDP to 3% by independence. Wealth extraction mechanisms included:

  • Trade surplus drain
  • Taxation funding British expenses
  • Export of raw materials
  • Suppression of local industries (textiles, handicrafts)

Famines & Starvation

Major famines were worsened or caused by British policies:

  • Bengal Famine 1943: 3–5 million deaths due to hoarding & export prioritization
  • Orissa Famine 1866: tens of thousands dead
  • Madras & Punjab famines in 19th century: preventable deaths from British neglect

Social & Cultural Disruption

British colonialism caused long-term social damage:

  • Codification of caste, strengthening rigid hierarchies
  • Divide-and-rule policies fostering religious and regional divisions
  • Collapse of local economies, artisan communities, and educational systems
  • Loss of indigenous knowledge and culture
