Introduction

This website exposes the brutal and shocking reality of British colonial rule in India. Millions died due to famine, forced labor, repression, and economic exploitation. While Germany paid reparations for Nazi crimes, India remains largely denied justice for the 45 trillion dollar loot and the death of millions under colonial rule.

Colonial Crimes

Forced Labor & Wars

Over 2 million Indians were conscripted in WWI, millions more in WWII. Many died due to harsh conditions, disease, and inadequate supplies.

Mass Killings & Repression

Jallianwala Bagh massacre (1919) alone killed 1,000+ unarmed civilians. 1857 revolt suppression caused hundreds of thousands deaths, entire villages destroyed.

Destruction of Local Economy

Handloom, textile, and artisan industries destroyed. Millions lost livelihoods; centuries-old skills vanished, replaced by dependence on imported British goods.

Famines & Starvation

Bengal Famine 1943 caused 3–5 million deaths due to hoarding, export prioritization, and British wartime policies. Millions died preventably.

Economic Loot

British systematically drained India’s wealth over 173 years:

  • Estimated £9.2 trillion (≈$45 trillion today) stolen (Utsa Patnaik)
  • Export of raw materials without fair payment
  • High taxation to fund British empire wars
  • Suppression of local industries (textiles, handicrafts)
  • Forced cash-crop cultivation, e.g., indigo, opium

Famines & Starvation

  • Bengal Famine 1943: 3–5 million deaths; rice exported to Britain & troops prioritized
  • Orissa Famine 1866: 100,000+ deaths due to government neglect
  • Madras & Punjab famines 19th century: preventable starvation intensified by colonial policies
  • Millions starved while grain was exported

Social & Cultural Disruption

  • Codification & rigidification of caste system
  • Divide-and-rule policies deepened religious & regional divisions
  • Collapse of local economies, artisan communities, traditional safety nets
  • Loss of indigenous knowledge, culture, and autonomy
